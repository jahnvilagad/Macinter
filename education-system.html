Education System

The educational system in the United Kingdom is slightly different from that in the United States and Taiwan. After completing compulsory education at the age of 16, students can choose "Further Education (FE)" to continue their academic development through A-level, IB, etc., and enter university; Choose technical and vocational education and enter the workplace after completing your degree. It usually takes only three years to obtain a British bachelor's degree, but some courses including internships and bachelor's degrees in Scotland are mainly four-year programs like Taiwan and the United States.

Since some pre-university courses are included in the expanded education, if Taiwanese high school students want to study in general universities in the United Kingdom, they usually need to apply for International Foundation Courses or International Year One to supplement their professional fields. The knowledge gap also allows students time to adapt to the British educational environment.
