Introduction to the academic system

The educational system in the United Kingdom is slightly different from that in the United States and Taiwan. After completing compulsory education at the age of 16, students can choose "Further Education (FE)" to continue their academic development through A-level, IB, etc., and enter university; Choose technical and vocational education and enter the workplace after completing your degree. It usually takes only three years to obtain a British bachelor's degree, but some courses including internships and bachelor's degrees in Scotland are mainly four-year programs like Taiwan and the United States.

Since some pre-university courses are included in the expanded education, if Taiwanese high school students want to study in general universities in the United Kingdom, they usually need to apply for International Foundation Courses or International Year One to supplement their professional fields. The knowledge gap also allows students time to adapt to the British educational environment.

Introduction

Introduction to the academic system

British education adopts the term system. A year is divided into three seasons: autumn, spring and summer. The admission time is mainly autumn, followed by spring. Students can choose according to their own plans. The most attractive thing about the British Institute is the one-year short-term program and its inclusiveness for applicants from different fields. According to the curriculum planning, British masters can be divided into teaching type and research type. In addition, most schools also have pre-master's courses designed for interdisciplinary students to help the connection of different majors and the improvement of English ability.

Most UK masters, whether taught or research-based, require the submission of a personal thesis in order to obtain a degree. However, with the transformation of many vocational education institutions into general universities in recent years, many schools also offer Project-Based or master's programs including internships, providing more choices for international students.

Research Master's

Research Master

Research Master's Research Master's programmes focus on individual independent research and are one to two years in length. Professors tend to be more in a consulting role, and most do not teach. The research component of this degree is relatively high. Usually, students with a master's degree by research will be transferred to a doctoral degree, and the research during the master's degree will be further completed at the doctoral level. Common research masters include MRes (Master of Research) and MPhil (Master of Philosophy).
